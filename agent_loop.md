# The Agent Loop — How AI Agents Think and Act

An AI agent does not just answer questions. It **observes** what is happening, **decides** what to do, **acts** on that decision, **receives feedback** on the result, and then **improves** its next action. This cycle repeats continuously — which is what makes it an agent loop.

---

## The 5 Steps of the Agent Loop

---

### Step 1 — Observe

The agent looks at available data and notices what is happening.

**Example — What did the agent observe about Ayesha?**

- Ayesha has **not logged in for 9 days**
- She has completed only **1 out of 4 assignments**
- Her last activity was over a week ago
- She is falling behind the expected pace of the course

The agent does not wait to be asked. It continuously monitors learner data and flags patterns that need attention.

---

### Step 2 — Decide

Based on what it observed, the agent decides what action is most likely to help.

**How the agent decided what to do:**

- A long gap in login + incomplete assignments = learner is at risk of dropping out
- The most effective first step is re-engagement, not punishment
- Sending a warm, encouraging reminder is better than a formal warning
- The agent selects the action with the highest chance of bringing Ayesha back

---

### Step 3 — Act

The agent carries out the decision it made.

**What action did the agent recommend first?**

- Sent Ayesha a **personalised reminder message**:
  *"Assalamu Alaikum Ayesha! We noticed you have been away for a few days. You have already completed 1 assignment — great start! Your next assignment is waiting. You can do this."*
- Flagged Ayesha's profile on the **coach dashboard** as needing follow-up
- Suggested to her coach: *"Consider reaching out to Ayesha this week"*

---

### Step 4 — Get Feedback

After taking action, the agent watches what happens next. The result of the action becomes feedback.

**What feedback did the agent receive?**

- Ayesha **did not log in** after the first reminder message
- No assignment was submitted within 48 hours
- The gentle reminder alone was not enough to re-engage her

This tells the agent that the first action did not work — and it needs to try something different.

---

### Step 5 — Improve

The agent uses the feedback to make a smarter decision next time.

**How did the agent improve the next recommendation?**

- Since the message did not work, the agent **escalated the response**
- It sent a second message with a **specific action step**:
  *"Ayesha, your Assignment 2 takes only 20 minutes. Try completing just the first section today."*
- It also **notified the coach directly**: *"Ayesha has not responded to an automated reminder. A personal call may be needed."*
- The agent updated Ayesha's risk profile from **medium** to **high priority**

The agent learned from the failed action and made a more targeted, more urgent response the second time.

---

## Full Agent Loop — Ayesha's Example at a Glance

| Step | What Happened |
|---|---|
| Observe | Ayesha has not logged in for 9 days. Only 1 of 4 assignments done. |
| Decide | Send a warm re-engagement reminder message |
| Act | Personalised reminder sent. Coach dashboard flagged. |
| Get Feedback | Ayesha did not log in. No assignment submitted in 48 hours. |
| Improve | Sent a more specific action-step message. Escalated to coach for personal follow-up. |

---

## How Is This Different from a Normal Chatbot?

A normal chatbot **waits** for you to ask a question and then gives a single answer. It has no memory of what happened before and takes no action on its own.

An AI agent **watches** what is happening without being asked, **decides** on the best action based on patterns and history, and **adjusts** its behaviour based on whether previous actions worked or not. It does not just respond — it **acts, learns, and improves** over time.

In Ayesha's case, a chatbot would only help if Ayesha opened it and typed a question. The agent noticed her absence on its own, reached out proactively, and changed its approach when the first attempt did not work — all without any human instruction.
